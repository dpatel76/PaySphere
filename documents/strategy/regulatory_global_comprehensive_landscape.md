## NORTH AMERICA

### 1. UNITED STATES

**Primary Regulator:** FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network)
**Central Bank:** Federal Reserve
**FIU:** FinCEN

#### Report Types:

##### 1.1 Currency Transaction Report (CTR) - Form 112
- **Trigger:** Cash transactions >$10,000 USD (single or aggregated in 24 hours)
- **Fields:** 104+ (ALREADY DOCUMENTED)
- **Filing:** BSA E-Filing System
- **Deadline:** 15 days from transaction

##### 1.2 Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) - Form 111
- **Trigger:** Suspicious activity regardless of amount (>$5,000 threshold for certain types)
- **Fields:** 110+ (ALREADY DOCUMENTED)
- **Filing:** BSA E-Filing System
- **Deadline:** 30 days from detection

##### 1.3 Report of International Transportation of Currency or Monetary Instruments (CMIR) - Form 105
- **Trigger:** Physical transport of >$10,000 across US border
- **Fields:** 35
- **Filing:** CBP (Customs and Border Protection)
- **Deadline:** At time of transport

##### 1.4 Report of Foreign Bank and Financial Accounts (FBAR) - FinCEN Form 114
- **Trigger:** US persons with foreign financial accounts >$10,000 aggregate
- **Fields:** 50+
- **Filing:** BSA E-Filing System
- **Deadline:** April 15 (individual), varies for entities

##### 1.5 OFAC Blocking Report
- **Trigger:** Blocking or rejecting transactions due to OFAC sanctions
- **Fields:** 25
- **Filing:** OFAC reporting portal
- **Deadline:** Within 10 days, annual report

##### 1.6 IRS Form 8300
- **Trigger:** Cash payments >$10,000 in trade or business
- **Fields:** 60
- **Filing:** IRS E-Filing
- **Deadline:** 15 days from receipt

##### 1.7 FATCA Reporting (Form 8966)
- **Trigger:** Foreign Financial Institution reporting on US account holders
- **Fields:** 80+
- **Filing:** IRS FATCA System
- **Deadline:** Annual (March 31)

##### 1.8 Federal Reserve Fedwire Activity Reports
- **Trigger:** Operational reporting on Fedwire usage
- **Fields:** 30
- **Filing:** Federal Reserve reporting system
- **Deadline:** Monthly

**Total US Fields:** 494+

### 2. CANADA

**Primary Regulator:** FINTRAC (Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre of Canada)
**Central Bank:** Bank of Canada
**FIU:** FINTRAC

#### Report Types:

##### 2.1 Suspicious Transaction Report (STR)
- **Trigger:** Reasonable grounds to suspect ML/TF
- **Fields:** 120
- **Key Differences from US SAR:** Includes attempted transactions, different activity codes
- **Filing:** FINTRAC Web Reporting System
- **Deadline:** 30 days from detection

##### 2.2 Large Cash Transaction Report (LCTR)
- **Trigger:** Cash transactions ≥CAD $10,000 (single transaction)
- **Fields:** 85
- **Key Differences from US CTR:** No aggregation, single transactions only
- **Filing:** FINTRAC Web Reporting System
- **Deadline:** 15 days from transaction

##### 2.3 Electronic Funds Transfer Report (EFT)
- **Trigger:** International EFTs ≥CAD $10,000
- **Fields:** 95
- **Sub-types:** EFTI (incoming), EFTO (outgoing)
- **Filing:** FINTRAC Web Reporting System
- **Deadline:** 5 business days

##### 2.4 Casino Disbursement Report
- **Trigger:** Casino disbursements ≥CAD $10,000
- **Fields:** 60
- **Filing:** FINTRAC Web Reporting System
- **Deadline:** 15 days

##### 2.5 Terrorist Property Report
- **Trigger:** Property owned/controlled by listed terrorist entities
- **Fields:** 40
- **Filing:** FINTRAC and RCMP
- **Deadline:** Immediately, monthly updates

**Total Canada Fields:** 400

### 5. EUROPEAN UNION (EU27 + EEA)

**Primary Framework:** 5AMLD/6AMLD, PSD2, GDPR
**Supervisors:** National Competent Authorities (NCAs) in each member state
**FIUs:** Each member state has own FIU

#### Report Types (Common across EU):

##### 5.1 Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) - Varies by Member State
- **Trigger:** Suspicion of ML/TF
- **Fields:** 100-150 (varies by member state)
- **Filing:** National FIU (e.g., TRACFIN in France, FIU-Deutschland, FIU-IND in Netherlands)
- **Deadline:** Immediately or within days (varies)

##### 5.2 PSD2 Fraud Reporting to EBA
- **Trigger:** Bi-annual aggregated fraud statistics
- **Fields:** 150+ (ALREADY DOCUMENTED)
- **Filing:** National competent authority → EBA
- **Deadline:** Bi-annual (H1 and H2)

##### 5.3 PSD2 Major Operational/Security Incidents
- **Trigger:** Major operational or security incident
- **Fields:** 60
- **Filing:** National competent authority
- **Deadline:** Within 4 hours of detection (initial), detailed report within 3 days

##### 5.4 SEPA Credit Transfer Recall Reporting
- **Trigger:** Recall requests for SCT/SCT Inst
- **Fields:** 40
- **Filing:** SEPA scheme adherence reporting
- **Deadline:** Monthly

##### 5.5 EU Common Reporting Standard (CRS) - Tax
- **Trigger:** Reportable accounts for tax residents of other jurisdictions
- **Fields:** 75
- **Filing:** National tax authority
- **Deadline:** Annual

**Key EU Member States with Additional Requirements:**

##### 5.6 Germany (BaFin, FIU-Deutschland)
- Suspected ML/TF reporting (Verdachtsmeldung): 140 fields
- Cash transaction monitoring (€10,000 threshold): 85 fields
- SEPA Instant Payment fraud reporting: 55 fields

##### 5.7 France (TRACFIN, ACPR)
- Déclaration de soupçon (Suspicious transaction declaration): 125 fields
- Large cash transaction declaration (€10,000): 80 fields

##### 5.8 Italy (UIF - Unità di Informazione Finanziaria)
- Segnalazione di operazione sospetta (SOS): 130 fields
- Aggregate suspicious activity reporting: 70 fields

##### 5.9 Spain (SEPBLAC)
- Comunicación por indicio (Suspicious activity communication): 120 fields
- High-value transaction reporting (€100,000): 75 fields

##### 5.10 Netherlands (FIU-Nederland)
- Unusual transaction report (MOT - Melding Ongebruikelijke Transacties): 135 fields

**Total EU Fields (aggregated across unique requirements):** 900+

## COMMON REGULATORY PATTERNS

### FATF 40 Recommendations - Common Framework

Most jurisdictions base their AML/CFT regulations on the **Financial Action Task Force (FATF) 40 Recommendations**, which creates commonalities:

#### Common Field Categories Across All Jurisdictions:

1. **Party Identification (Debtor/Originator)**
   - Full name (35-40 fields)
   - Address (15-20 fields)
   - Identification documents (10-15 fields)
   - Birth date, place, nationality (5-8 fields)
   - Occupation, employer (3-5 fields)
   - **Total:** 70-90 fields per party

2. **Party Identification (Creditor/Beneficiary)**
   - Same as above: 70-90 fields

3. **Financial Institution Information**
   - Name, BIC/SWIFT, address, identifiers (25-35 fields per FI)
   - Typically 2-4 FIs per transaction (debtor agent, creditor agent, intermediaries)
   - **Total:** 75-140 fields

4. **Transaction Information**
   - Amount, currency, exchange rate (5-10 fields)
   - Transaction type, method, channel (5-8 fields)
   - Dates (execution, value, settlement) (3-6 fields)
   - Purpose, narrative (2-5 fields)
   - **Total:** 20-30 fields

5. **Account Information**
   - Account number, type, currency (5-10 fields per account)
   - Typically 2 accounts (debtor, creditor)
   - **Total:** 10-20 fields

6. **Reporting Entity Information**
   - Institution name, identifier, branch, contact (10-20 fields)

7. **Report Metadata**
   - Report type, reference, submission date, status (10-15 fields)

8. **Suspicious Activity Details** (for STR/SAR)
   - Activity types/typologies (10-20 fields)
   - Narrative (1 large text field)
   - Dates, amounts, patterns (10-15 fields)
   - **Total:** 25-40 fields

**Common Base:** 280-430 fields per jurisdiction for comprehensive reporting

**Jurisdiction-Specific Additions:** 50-200 additional fields for local requirements
